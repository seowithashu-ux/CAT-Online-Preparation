## Step 1: Understand the Exam Structure Clearly

Before starting preparation, develop a clear understanding of:

- Sectional time limits  
- Question distribution trends  
- Difficulty level variations  
- Marking scheme  

Clarity about the exam structure prevents wasted effort and ensures focused preparation.



## Step 2: Build Strong Fundamentals (First 3–4 Months)

Your foundation phase should focus on concept clarity across all three sections.

### VARC
- Develop a daily reading habit (editorials, business articles)  
- Practice RC passages with deep analysis  
- Strengthen grammar and para-jumbles  

### DILR
- Solve 2–3 quality sets daily  
- Focus on understanding puzzle structures  
- Improve set selection skills  

### QA
- Start with Arithmetic fundamentals  
- Move to Algebra and Geometry gradually  
- Maintain a formula notebook

## Step 3: Sectional Testing & Time Optimization

Once basics are strong, shift to sectional mock testing:

- Take timed sectional tests weekly  
- Track accuracy percentage  
- Identify slow-solving areas  
- Improve question selection strategy  

Consistency at this stage builds performance stability — essential for high percentiles.



## Step 4: Full-Length Mock Strategy (Last 3 Months)

Mocks should simulate real exam pressure.

Follow this structured system:

- Start with 1 mock per week  
- Increase to 2–3 mocks per week closer to the exam  
- Spend double the test time on analysis  
- Maintain an error log  

iQuanta’s CAT online preparation course offers advanced mock analytics, percentile benchmarking, and detailed solutions that convert practice into measurable improvement.



## Step 5: Smart Revision Framework

Revision ensures consistency and retention.

- Weekly formula revision  
- Reattempt mistakes from previous mocks  
- Strengthen weak topics  
- Practice mixed sectional drills  

Structured revision keeps concepts fresh and execution sharp.

## Step 6: Final 30-Day Sprint Plan

The last month before CAT should focus on:

- High-quality mock practice  
- Accuracy improvement  
- Stress management  
- Strength-based attempt strategy  

Avoid learning new concepts during this phase. Focus on refining strengths and eliminating recurring errors.
